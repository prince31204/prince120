Exploring Space with ISRO

Introduction to ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India, headquartered in Bengaluru. It was founded in 1969 with the vision to harness space technology for national development while pursuing space science research and planetary exploration.

ISRO's Achievements

ISRO has achieved several milestones in space exploration, including:

  • Launch of Chandrayaan-1, India's first lunar probe, in 2008.
  • Successful Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) in 2013, making India the first country to reach Mars orbit in its maiden attempt.
  • Launch of a record 104 satellites in a single mission aboard the PSLV-C37 rocket in 2017.

Future Endeavors

ISRO has ambitious plans for the future, including:

  • Chandrayaan-3 mission to explore the Moon's south pole.
  • Gaganyaan mission, India's first manned space mission, aiming to send astronauts to space.
